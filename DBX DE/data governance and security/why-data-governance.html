Data Governance · The "Why"

Why Do We Need Data Governance?

Analytics, engineering, and science all use data. Governance is what keeps that data findable, trustworthy, secure, and accountable as your platform grows — and Unity Catalog is where you do it.

Discoverability Access control Lineage Auditing Quality Security
In one sentence: Data governance is the set of rules, processes, and tools that make sure the right people can find, trust, use, and safely access the right data — and that you can always prove who did what with it. It's the discipline that lets a data solution scale and survive after the exciting build phase is over.

4 · Why it maps to Unity Catalog

This is why you're "already doing governance." Per Databricks' docs, Unity Catalog is the unified governance layer built into Databricks — it sits beneath every data interaction and automatically enforces access control when you query a table, tracks lineage as data moves, and logs activity for auditing. It governs structured and unstructured data, plus AI assets like ML models.

Instead of bolting on six separate tools for six pillars, Unity Catalog gives you one place for discovery, access control, lineage, auditing, and quality — which is why it's described as a one-stop governance solution.

5 · Why data engineers especially own it

You build the data that analysts and scientists consume. That puts you closest to the hard governance questions:

  • You decide structure, naming, and where data lives → you shape discoverability.
  • You build the pipelines → you're responsible for lineage and quality.
  • You grant and design access → you own a big part of access control and security.

It's everyone's responsibility, but the engineer is closest to the data — so the questions land on your desk first.

6 · Common mistakes & gotchas

Governance as an afterthought. Bolting it on after the platform is built is painful. Design the catalog/schema structure and access model from day one.
"We have a catalog" ≠ "we have governance." The catalog is the tool; governance is the practice of using it consistently — naming standards, ownership, tagging.
Over-locking or under-locking access. Too tight blocks productivity; too loose causes breaches. Least-privilege plus groups is the balance.

7 · Quick recap

  • Governance = find, trust, use, secure, and account for data over time.
  • Without it: duplicate work, distrust, broken downstreams, breaches, failed audits.
  • It's a bundle of pillars — discoverability, access control, lineage, auditing, quality, security.
  • Unity Catalog is the single, built-in governance layer for all of them.
  • Data engineers carry the most responsibility because they build the data.
