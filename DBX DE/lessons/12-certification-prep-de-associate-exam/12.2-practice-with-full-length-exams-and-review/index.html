Practice Exams & Review Weak Areas

Take full, timed practice exams; score by domain; review only the weak domains by expected-gain; re-test until consistently passing. Then book.

The one-liner

Take full-length, timed practice exams, score by the 7 domains, then review your weak domains ranked by gap × weight (redo those lessons/notebooks), and re-test until you're consistently ~80%+ everywhere.
✈️ Analogy: a flight simulator before the real flight — rehearse the exact conditions (timing, phrasing, pressure), see where you crash, and fix those maneuvers, not the ones you already nail.

How it works — deep dive

1 · Where to get legitimate practice

Mechanism: the official Databricks practice exam + Academy track the current 7-domain blueprint and naming. Trade-off: third-party "dumps" are often stale/wrong and risk the exam agreement — verify everything against docs.

✅ Official practice exam (Databricks certification page)
✅ Databricks Academy / self-paced DE learning path
✅ The official exam guide (domain list + weights)
⚠️ Third-party "dumps": verify every claim against docs — often stale or wrong.

2 · Simulate real conditions (timed, full length)

Mechanism: sit a full 45-question set in one 90-minute block, no notes, no pausing. Trade-off: untimed practice inflates your score and hides the real failure mode — running out of time.

Mock-exam rules:  45 questions · 90:00 timer · no notes · no pausing
Pacing target:    ~2 min/question; flag hard ones, answer all (no blank — no penalty)

3 · The weak-area review loop

Mechanism: score by domain, map gaps to Stages 1–11, drill the worst score-vs-weight payoff first, re-test. Trade-off: re-studying comfortable strong domains feels good but barely moves the score.

1. Baseline   → full timed exam BEFORE heavy study
2. Score      → per-domain %, mapped to the 7 domains (12.1) + your stages
3. Prioritize → fix lowest score × highest weight first (biggest point gain)
4. Drill      → redo that stage's lesson + consolidated notebook
5. Re-test    → another full exam; confirm gain, check no regression

4 · Worked example — diagnosing a weak domain

Mechanism: turn a score into an action by ranking gap × weight (expected score gain). Trade-off: sequence by expected gain, not by anxiety.

# Scores: Govern 45, Optim 60, Jobs 76, CI/CD 72, Transform 82, Ingest 88, Platform 90
Governance:   (100-45) × 0.15 = 8.25   ← study FIRST  (Stage 8/9)
Optimization: (100-60) × 0.10 = 4.00   ← then this    (Stage 3/7/10)
Jobs:         (100-76) × 0.16 = 3.84   ← then this    (Stage 7)
CI/CD:        (100-72) × 0.10 = 2.80   ← then this    (Stage 11)

5 · Rehearse the question style (samples + rationale)

Mechanism: items are short scenarios with code/config; pick the option that's correct AND current. Trade-off: internalize why the right option wins — don't memorize snippets.

-- Q: "Insert new rows AND update changed rows in one statement." → MERGE.
MERGE INTO main.silver.customers t USING updates s ON t.id = s.id
WHEN MATCHED THEN UPDATE SET * WHEN NOT MATCHED THEN INSERT *;
-- Why: MERGE = upsert. INSERT OVERWRITE replaces; INSERT INTO only appends.
# Q: "Ingest only new files as they land, tracking schema." → Auto Loader.
spark.readStream.format("cloudFiles") \
  .option("cloudFiles.format", "csv") \
  .option("cloudFiles.schemaLocation", "/Volumes/main/raw/_schema") \
  .load("/Volumes/main/raw/in")
# Why: cloudFiles = Auto Loader (incremental + schema). spark.read reprocesses all;
#      COPY INTO is SQL-batch, not a stream.
# Q: "Run a job every day at 02:00." → 6-field Quartz, not 5-field Unix.
quartz_cron_expression: "0 0 2 * * ?"    # ✅ sec min hour day month dow
"0 2 * * *"                              # ❌ 5-field Unix cron — wrong format

Why it matters

  • Reading ≠ recalling under time pressure — practice exams turn passive knowledge into test-ready recall and surface blind spots.
  • 45 Q / 90 min ≈ 2 min each — timed practice trains pacing so you don't fail knowable questions by running out of time.
🎯 Real-world use case: score 60% — Governance 40%, Ingestion 85%. Spend the next sessions on Stage 8 (governance) and re-test, rather than re-reading Delta basics you've mastered.

Uses, edge cases & limitations

  • Uses: finding blind spots, building pacing/stamina, an objective "am I ready to book?" signal.
  • Edge cases: don't memorize answers (the real exam rewords); one high score isn't enough — look for consistency across domains + margin.
  • Limitation: third-party "dumps" may be outdated/inaccurate (wrong Lakeflow naming, retired features) — prefer the official practice exam + current guide; verify against docs.

Common gotchas

  • ❌ Memorizing practice questions instead of understanding the concept.
  • ❌ Practicing untimed, then running out of time on the real exam.
  • ❌ Re-studying strong domains because they feel comfortable.
  • ❌ Trusting out-of-date dumps — names/features change (DLT → Lakeflow SDP).
  • ❌ Booking off one good score instead of consistent ~80%+ with margin.
